WHAT IS CANSAT?

The UCI CanSat team is a senior design project that competes in the international CanSat competition. Each year, we design, manufacture, and launch a satellite payload model according to a set of mission objectives and requirements set for that specific year. In previous years, we have designed a foldable delta-winged glider, maple seed payloads aimed at taking barometric data, and a camera payload that maintained its video orientation tethered to the container throughout the whole flight. Through these achievements, we were able to secure our second Dean’s Choice award during the 2022 Winter Design Review. We have also secured sponsorships and mentorships from large companies such as Blue Origin.

Our team is relatively small (10 engineering members maximum) which gives each member opportunities to learn and directly contribute to our project. We spend Fall Quarter designing mechanical components in SolidWorks, selecting electrical components, and prototyping the ground station. In Winter Quarter, we manufacture mechanical prototypes, iterate on the PCB design, and begin work on programming the MCU. We also prepare for the Preliminary Design Review (PDR) and Critical Design Review (CDR). In Spring Quarter, we complete the final build and conduct tests, and we attend the CanSat competition, which usually takes place in Virginia at the end of the Quarter.

If you want to learn more, we encourage you to visit the competition website at http://www.cansatcompetition.com and our website at http://cansat.eng.uci.edu. In addition, feel free to email us at cansatuci@gmail.com. We are excited to welcome new brilliant minds onto our team and hope you apply.

MECHANICAL ENGINEER

DESCRIPTION

Work with Mechanical/Aerospace teammates to design, fabricate, assemble, and test the mechanical structure, mechanisms, and descent control devices of the CanSat. Fall Quarter is focused on creating the mechanical design in Solidworks. Most work is done individually in Solidworks with weekly mechanical subteam meetings. Winter Quarter is focused on 3D printing and manufacturing the CanSat, as well as testing the mechanisms. Team members attend scheduled lab times to start prints and test and assemble prototypes. Spring Quarter is devoted to design iteration and testing, as well as finalizing the CanSat for the competition. Most of the time is spent working on assembly and testing in the lab. Environmental tests including a vacuum test, thermal test, vibration test, and drop test are performed.

REQUIREMENTS

  • Recommended Majors: Mechanical Engineering, Aerospace Engineering
  • Recommended Courses: ENGR 7A/B, MAE 106, MAE 52, MAE 189, MAE 195, MAE 30, MAE 80, MAE 130A/130B
  • Solidworks experience (3D modeling) (required)
  • 3D printing and manufacturing experience
  • Plan to learn through the process if no prior experience
  • Knowledge of basic aerodynamics
  • Leadership and time management skills

ELECTRICAL ENGINEER

DESCRIPTION

Work on developing the CanSat’s Electrical subsystem through Printed Circuit Board (PCB) development. Responsibilities include

  • Selecting appropriate batteries and power components to ensure optimal performance and support for all sensors and mechanical hardware
  • Designing and developing custom CanSat PCBs, focusing on efficient layout, component selection, and power management
  • Managing PCB manufacturing, assembly, and testing processes
  • Testing and troubleshooting wireless data transmission and antenna performance during Winter and Spring Quarter
  • Coordinating with team members to integrate electrical systems with mechanical and software components
  • Performing hardware testing and troubleshooting to identify and resolve issues in the design and assembly process.

REQUIREMENTS

  • Recommended Majors: Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, Computer Science and Engineering
  • Required Courses: EECS 70A
  • Useful Courses: EECS 70B, EECS 50 or 150, EECS 10
  • Experience with a PCB/electrical design program (or willingness to learn quickly)
  • Good soldering and electrical troubleshooting skills
  • Experience using lab equipment such as oscilloscopes and function generators
  • Applicants with previous electrical design and manufacturing experience will be preferred

EMBEDDED SOFTWARE ENGINEER

DESCRIPTION

Collaborate with mechanical and electrical teams to develop various software systems for CanSat, including Ground Station, Flight Software, and Test Benches. Key responsibili

  • Designing and implementing a Ground Station (a control unit) for the CanSat to communicate with the CanSat, send commands to it, and display data received
  • Working with the mechanical team to test various components of the CanSat through Arduino code snippets
  • Collaborating with the electrical team to design a test bench to ensure the functionality of the CanSat PCB and components
  • Developing embedded systems for the Payload of the CanSat which will be based on an MCU such as STM32 or ESP32
  • Coordinating with team members to test and debug issues
  • Performing integration testing to ensure functionality of the CanSat

REQUIREMENTS

  • Recommended Majors: Computer Science, Computer Science and Engineering, Computer Engineering, Electrical Engineering
  • Useful courses: COMPSCI 145, COMPSCI 147, EECS 20
  • Experience with C or C++ Embedded Programming (required)
  • Strong familiarity and experience with Git (required)
  • Experience with communication protocols (e.g. I2C)
  • Experience with radio communication preferred
